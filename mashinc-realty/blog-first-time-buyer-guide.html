First-Time Home Buyer's Complete Guide

November 18, 2024 • Buyer's Guide • By Abayomi Animashaun

Buying your first home in Nigeria is an exciting milestone, but it can also feel overwhelming. This comprehensive guide will walk you through every step of the process, from preparation to closing, ensuring you make informed decisions and avoid common pitfalls.

Phase 1: Preparation & Planning

Assess Your Financial Readiness

Income Requirements:

  • • Stable employment for 2+ years
  • • Debt-to-income ratio below 40%
  • • Monthly income 3x mortgage payment
  • • Consistent salary history

Savings Needed:

  • • Down payment (10-30% of property value)
  • • Legal fees (2-5% of property value)
  • • Survey and valuation costs
  • • 3-6 months emergency fund

Credit Score Optimization

A good credit score significantly improves your mortgage terms and approval chances.

Excellent: 750+
Best rates available
Good: 650-749

Phase 2: Understanding Financing Options

Primary Mortgage Banks (PMBs)

Interest Rate: 18-25% per annum
Loan Tenure: 5-20 years
Down Payment: 20-30%

Best for: Traditional buyers seeking established mortgage products

Commercial Banks

Interest Rate: 20-28% per annum
Loan Tenure: 5-15 years
Down Payment: 30-50%

Best for: Existing customers with strong banking relationships

Phase 3: Property Search & Selection

Define Your Requirements

Must-Haves:

  • • Number of bedrooms/bathrooms
  • • Maximum commute time to work
  • • School district quality
  • • Security features
  • • Parking requirements

Nice-to-Haves:

  • • Swimming pool or gym
  • • Garden or balcony
  • • Modern kitchen appliances
  • • Generator backup
  • • Shopping centers nearby

Location Evaluation Checklist

Infrastructure

  • □ Road quality & accessibility
  • □ Power supply reliability
  • □ Water supply consistency
  • □ Internet connectivity

Amenities

  • □ Healthcare facilities
  • □ Educational institutions
  • □ Shopping centers
  • □ Recreation facilities

Safety & Security

  • □ Crime rates in area
  • □ Security personnel
  • □ Street lighting
  • □ Gated community

Phase 4: Legal Due Diligence

⚠️ Critical Document Verification

Never proceed without verifying these essential documents. Many buyers lose money due to fake or disputed titles.

Essential Documents to Verify

1

Certificate of Occupancy (C of O)

Government-issued title proving ownership rights

2

Survey Plan

Registered survey showing property boundaries

3

Deed of Assignment

Document transferring ownership from seller to buyer

4

Tax Clearance Certificate

Proof of paid property taxes

5

Building Approval Plan

Government approval for construction

Phase 5: Closing the Deal

Final Steps Timeline

1-7

Days 1-7: Final Documentation

  • • Complete mortgage application
  • • Submit all required documents
  • • Schedule property inspection
  • • Finalize insurance arrangements
8-14

Days 8-14: Bank Processing

  • • Bank conducts property valuation
  • • Credit assessment completion
  • • Mortgage offer letter issued
  • • Legal documentation review
15-21

Days 15-21: Final Closing

  • • Sign deed of assignment
  • • Transfer property ownership
  • • Complete payment transactions
  • • Receive keys and possession

Total Closing Costs Breakdown

Mandatory Costs:

  • • Legal fees: 2-5% of property value
  • • Stamp duty: 3-6% of property value
  • • Survey fees: ₹150,000 - ₦300,000
  • • Registration fees: ₦50,000 - ₦200,000

Additional Costs:

  • • Property insurance: 0.5-1% annually
  • • Moving expenses: ₦100,000 - ₦500,000
  • • Utility connections: ₦200,000 - ₦500,000
  • • Immediate repairs: 2-5% of property value

Total Expected Closing Costs: 8-15% of property purchase price

Phase 6: Post-Purchase Essentials

First Month Priorities

Update address with bank and employer
Set up utility accounts and connections
Complete property insurance activation
Schedule professional property inspection
Create maintenance schedule and budget

Long-term Wealth Building

  • • Property Appreciation: Track market value annually
  • • Tax Benefits: Maintain records for deductions
  • • Equity Building: Consider extra principal payments
  • • Future Investment: Plan for additional properties
